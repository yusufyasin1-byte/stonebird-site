Spend Scout vs YNAB

A YNAB alternative for people who do not want bank sync.

YNAB is a mature budgeting system with a full method and broader planning workflow. Spend Scout is narrower: private iPhone expense tracking, manual-first logging, Apple Pay Shortcuts capture, and a simple month-end forecast without linking your bank.

Get launch notice Spend Scout overview

Quick answer

Should you use YNAB or Spend Scout?

Use YNAB if you want a complete budgeting method, planning workflow, and mature ecosystem. Consider Spend Scout if your problem is simpler: you want to know where this month is heading, log expenses quickly, and avoid giving a budgeting app your bank login.

Source note: this page links to YNAB's official website. YNAB is independent from Stonebird Labs.

Comparison

Different tools for different budgeting jobs.

Best fit YNAB: people who want a full budgeting system and budgeting method. Spend Scout: people who want private expense tracking and a forecast without bank login.
Main job Plan money, assign dollars, and manage a broader budget workflow. Log spending, check pace, and see where the month is likely to end.
Bank connection Useful if you want connected-account workflows. Not required. Spend Scout is built around no bank login and manual-first capture.

Decision guide

Choose based on the behavior you want.

Choose YNAB for structure

If you want a full budgeting philosophy and a mature planning workflow, YNAB is the stronger fit.

Choose Spend Scout for privacy

If your hard requirement is no bank login and no account aggregation, Spend Scout is built around that constraint.

Choose Spend Scout for pace

If the question is "am I on pace this month?", Spend Scout keeps the answer visible without turning budgeting into a large system.
