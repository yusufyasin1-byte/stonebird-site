## Gridlock: Terminal — Competitive ASO Analysis & Action Plan

### Competitor Landscape

The air traffic control game category on the App Store is dominated by simulation-heavy titles with bloated file sizes and aggressive monetization. Gridlock occupies a unique pocket: fast-paced arcade gameplay, zero ads, zero IAP, and a tiny footprint. Here's how the competition breaks down.

**Unmatched Air Traffic Control** — 529 ratings, 4.4★, 2 GB, subscription model. Positioned as a hardcore simulator. Reviews consistently complain about predatory paywalls. Category: Simulation.

**Air Traffic Control: ATC Game** (Rabbit Mountain) — Free, subtitle "The flight control simulator." Speech-based controls and weather zones are its differentiator. Category: Simulation.

**Air Traffic Control 2D** (Appscraft) — 81 ratings, 4.0★, 209 MB, subtitle "Run the Control Tower." 30+ vehicle types, real-world maps. Category: Casual.

**World of Airports** — 14K ratings, 4.6★, 2.4 GB, subtitle "Airport & Airline Manager." Different genre entirely — management sim, not real-time routing.

**Planes Control.Air traffic ATC** — 620 ratings, 4.7★, 38 MB, subtitle "Simple flight simulator game." 60+ aircraft, free with IAP. Wastes the word "simple" in its subtitle.

**Key takeaway:** No competitor uses "arcade" anywhere in their metadata. Gridlock owns this keyword uncontested. Every single competitor has IAP, subscriptions, or ads — "free with no ads and no in-app purchases" is a powerful differentiator buried in your current positioning.

### Optimized Metadata Recommendations

**Title (30 char max):** Keep as is.
`Gridlock: Terminal`

The title is already clean and memorable. "Gridlock" is brandable and unique — no one else uses it. "Terminal" signals the air traffic theme. Don't dilute this with keyword stuffing.

**Subtitle (30 char max) — Recommended:**
`Air Traffic Control Arcade`

This is 28 characters and captures the two highest-value keywords you're currently missing: "air traffic control" (the #1 category search term) and "arcade" (your uncontested differentiator). Your current subtitle "Simulation" wastes the entire field on a single generic word that doesn't match the game's actual feel.

**Keyword Field (100 char max) — Recommended:**
`plane,flight,landing,airport,tower,routing,puzzle,offline,daily,reflex,game center,leaderboard,ios`

This is 93 characters. Every word is unique (no repeats from title or subtitle — Apple ignores duplicates). Single words separated by commas, no spaces. Covers the full search surface: people searching for "plane game," "flight game," "landing game," "airport game," "tower game," "puzzle game," "offline game," "daily game," and long-tail combinations.

**Localization Keyword Fields (the 300+ character bonus):**

Apple indexes keyword fields from all English-speaking locales for US App Store searches. This means you can add a completely separate keyword field for UK English, Australian English, Canadian English, and Irish English — and every word indexes for American searchers too.

- **en-GB:** `aeroplane,runway,controller,radar,approach,departure,hold,stack,clearance,taxiway,gatwick,heathrow`
- **en-AU:** `melbourne,sydney,qantas,flight path,approach control,ground control,airspace,circuit,landing strip`
- **en-CA:** `toronto,pearson,nav can,yvr,airspace,glideslope,transponder,mayday,emergency landing,turbulence`
- **en-IE:** `shannon,dublin,cork airport,ryanair,aer lingus,holding pattern,visual approach,final approach`

That's an additional ~400 characters of unique keyword coverage for free.

### Review Velocity

App Store ranking factors weigh review volume and recency. Implement `SKStoreReviewController` to prompt after positive moments: a new high score, a streak of clean landings, or after completing a Daily Dispatch. Apple limits prompts to 3 per year per user, so time them carefully — never after a crash.

---

### In-App Events

Apple's In-App Events appear in search results and can be featured editorially. Create events for:

- **Seasonal events:** "Holiday Rush — survive the busiest skies of the year"
- **Milestone events:** "1,000 Daily Dispatches completed — can you beat today's?"
- **Challenge events:** "Zero Crash Week — land every aircraft for 7 days straight"

These give Apple's editorial team reasons to feature you and add fresh metadata without a new build.
